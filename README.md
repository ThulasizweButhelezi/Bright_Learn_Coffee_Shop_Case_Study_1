# Bright_Learn_Coffee_Shop_Case_Study_1
Purpose
Bright Coffee Shop has appointed a new CEO focused on growing revenue and improving product performance through data‑driven decision‑making. The case study tasks you, as a Junior Data Analyst, with extracting actionable insights from historical daily sales data to support this goal.

Objectives
The analysis aims to:
• Identify which products generate the most revenue
• Determine peak sales periods (time of day and day of week)
• Examine sales trends across products and time intervals
• Provide strategic recommendations to improve sales performance

Tools & Platforms
You can use a combination of:
• Data planning tools: Miro and Canva
• Data processing tools: Databricks, SQL
• Visualization tools: Excel
• Presentation tools: PowerPoint, Canva

Key Tasks
1. Planning & Architecture (Miro and Canva)
• Design a data flow diagram showing data sources, ETL process, storage, and analysis.
• Define key insights and calculations (e.g., total amount = unit price × quantity).
2. Data Processing (Databricks)
• Clean and transform the dataset.
• Create time‑bucket columns ( 3‑hour intervals).
• Compute total amounts and group data by product and time.
3. Data Analysis (Excel)
• Build dashboards and pivot tables showing:
	◦ Revenue per product type
	◦ Peak time intervals
	◦ Quantity sold by category
	◦ Best‑selling products
4. Presentation to the CEO
• Prepare a methodology document and a visual presentation summarizing:
	◦ Key insights
	◦ Recommendations

 Dashboard Insights
1. Overall Revenue Performance
• Total revenue: Approximately R698,812.35 generated in the first six months.
• Trend: A steady upward trajectory from April to June, indicating growing customer engagement and effective product positioning.
2. Product Category Insights
• Top performers: Coffee and Tea dominate, contributing nearly 55%–60% of total revenue.
• Moderate performers: Bakery, Drinking Chocolate, and Coffee Beans show stable but slower growth — ideal for targeted promotions.
• Underperformers: Packaged Chocolate and Flavours contribute the least, suggesting potential for product redesign or bundling.
3. Time‑of‑Day Performance
• Morning (31%) and Early Morning (21%) are the strongest revenue periods — driven by commuter and breakfast demand.
• Afternoon (23%) shows moderate sales, while Evening (13%) and Mid‑Day/Lunch (12%) are weaker, offering opportunities for time‑specific campaigns.
4. Day‑of‑Week Performance
• Weekdays outperform weekends, with Monday (15%) leading.
• Saturday and Sunday are the lowest‑earning days — ideal for weekend‑specific promotions or events.
5. Store Location Insights
• Hell’s Kitchen leads with 34% of total revenue, followed closely by Astoria and Lower Manhattan.
• This suggests operational or marketing practices at Hell’s Kitchen could be replicated across other locations.
6. Sales Trends
• Coffee and Tea show consistent growth from January to June.
• Bakery and Chocolate remain steady, indicating loyal but limited customer segments.
• Seasonal or promotional factors likely influenced the April–June surge.

Key Takeaways
• Focus marketing and staffing on morning and weekday peaks.
• Expand coffee and tea lines with premium or seasonal offerings.
• Introduce weekend experiences to lift low‑performing days.
• Replicate Hell’s Kitchen’s success model across other stores.
• Use bundling and cross‑selling to boost moderate categories.
